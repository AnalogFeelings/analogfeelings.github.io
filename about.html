About Page

This website went through 3 redesigns, the first 2 designs used a library called XP.css. Now I use my own custom design.
It's pretty lightweight, and I tried to keep JavaScript usage to minimum.

Well, let's get to the point.
This website is a nicer hub for my GitHub profile.

  • The "Home" page is, well, the home tab. The main "entrance", if you will.
  • The "Posts" page will pretty much have random blog posts about random things that I decide to randomly write about at random intervals.
  • The "Knowledge" page is a sort of container for knowledge about obscure things, which I barely come across.
  • The "Projects" page will contain an organized tree view of my projects.
  • And the "About" page is what you are reading now!
Anyways, this as I said before, this website was built using something called XP.css during its first 2 designs.
Now you may be asking, "but why did you remove it? It looked so unique!". Well, it wasn't very mobile-friendly.
Hell, it wasn't even small-screen friendly! It also suffered from issues such as barely readable fonts, half-pixel scaling weirdness, and other garbage.

So instead, I decided to make my own design FROM SCRATCH. Yes, I also wanted to keep it relatively retro.
And after many days of extreme hyperfixation, here it is! The website you're looking at right now.

Isn't it cool? It even has mobile support! I know it's not something to brag about nowadays, but it is for me.

In Memory of Ekaterina "BerkeliumAsset"

You know, I'm not good at writing memorials or anything of that kind, but if someone deserves a memorial, it's our friend BerkeliumAsset.
On the 25th of November 2021, CaptainCrunch received a WhatsApp message from Berk's mother telling us about her suicide.
At first, I thought it was some sort of prank, but sadly, it wasn't. I spent some time afterward thinking about her, our conversations, and everything surrounding her.

I'm sorry I didn't know how to reply to your call for help, I didn't know that it even was one.
To this day, I still feel kind of guilty about it, maybe I could have stopped you, and I'm so sorry. You helped me so much through sad times, you even helped me discover my gender identity, and I'm truly grateful for that.

Wherever you are now, I'm hoping it's a happier place, I really hope it is.
As of writing this, I'm almost 1 year into HRT, and Crunch has been having some rough times, but we are managing to pull through, although it has been very hard for me.
I'm going to be completely honest, I don't remember most of my interactions with you, as my medication has made me lose a lot of my memories before 2024, but I know that I felt safe, comfortable, and even happy when talking to you.
I'm sorry, I am so sorry that I didn't help you. I hope you can forgive me, as I find it hard to forgive myself.

We will never forget you, Berk. I miss you so much.

May you rest in peace.
